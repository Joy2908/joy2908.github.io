Youtube UX Research

Project Overview

The main goal of this UX study project is to improve the YouTube user experience by finding and fixing the most important usability issues. We looked at how people use YouTube and a lot of different kinds of content. Because of this, the UX can use design improvements to create a better experience for both viewers and content creators. The main goal of this project is to find problems with annoying ads, video recommendations, and user interface issues. We also want to make finding user research, making testing, and analyzing easier. This will help us come up with good design solutions that make the platform easier to use overall.

Assumption

What are the users doing before using the app?

Users may be engaging in various activities such as seeking information, needing entertainment, or looking for relaxation; some might have a question they want answered, be on a break, or commuting.




What do users feel when using the app?

Users feel entertained and relaxed when they watch content that distracts them from daily stress, such as funny videos or relaxing vlogs.

Users feel connected when they interact with content creators or the community in comments, feeling like part of a group with shared interests.

Users feel motivated when watching videos that inspire personal growth, productivity, or goal-setting, such as fitness or study vlogs.

Users feel guilty or unproductive if they spend too much time watching videos, especially if they originally intended to watch “just one.”

Users feel encouraged to try new things after watching tutorial videos, recipes, or DIY guides that make activities seem achievable.

Users often find unskippable or repetitive ads disruptive, especially when watching shorter videos.

Some users dislike when content is removed, censored, or restricted without clear justification.




What are the behaviours of the users?

Subscribers frequently revisit channels they like, creating a sense of loyalty and routine in their viewing habits.

Users often scroll through recommended videos on the homepage, influenced by the algorithm to discover new content.

Many users go straight to the search bar to find tutorials, answers, or particular videos on topics of interest.

Many users try to avoid ads, either by using ad blockers, skipping ads, or subscribing to YouTube Premium for uninterrupted viewing.

Some users re-watch certain videos, especially tutorials, music, or inspirational content they want to revisit.







Final Three Assumption:

Many users try to avoid ads, either by using ad blockers, skipping ads, or subscribing to YouTube Premium for uninterrupted viewing.

Users feel motivated when watching videos that inspire personal growth, productivity, or goal-setting, such as fitness or study vlogs.

Users feel guilty or unproductive if they spend too much time watching videos, especially if they originally intended to watch “just one.”

Analysis

Key Insight

  • When users see ads on their videos, many of them will skip the ads if given the option.
  • Users are concerned about the cost of YouTube Premium, feeling that it’s a waste of money if they’re only subscribing to skip ads, and it's not worth the money. They are willing to be patient and skip ads instead of paying.
  • Users find ads to be annoying, particularly when they appear frequently or are unskippable.
  • Users enjoy 5-second ads because they are brief, but they dislike ads that are 20 seconds or longer, especially when they cannot skip them.
  • Users say that they feel motivated at first when watching educational or self-improvement content, but they tend to forget what they learned over time.
  • Users do not feel guilty if they end up watching more YouTube videos than they originally planned.
  • Users frequently listen to music on YouTube while they are working, studying, or doing housework, finding it helpful for staying focused.
  • Some users feel that extended time spent on YouTube can be a waste time, especially if it affects their productivity.

Definition

Goal

Our mission is to improve user pleasure on YouTube by decreasing commercial interruptions through points, promoting content that promotes personal growth and productivity, and adding features that support thoughtful viewing habits. This will build a more engaging and purposeful platform that aligns with users’ needs and aspirations.

Vision

Transforming YouTube into a platform that not only entertains but also makes users more conscious about how long they spend time on the platform while give the users the opportunity to provide an uninterrupted, motivating, and mindful viewing experience. By tackling disruptive ads through reward points at the same time the platform can increase the number of viewers, curating content that inspires personal growth, and promoting mindful screen time management tools, YouTube will become a space where users can seamlessly balance productivity and enjoyment.

Personas

Strategy

Strategy Statement

My goal is to help improve the YouTube user experience by addressing the problem of disruptive ads, promoting and boosting content, creating a feature that can create your own playlists and display them to users, and encouraging mindful viewing habits to help users avoid wasting time or feeling guilty about watching for long periods of time. This will encourage how we handle ads that can be more engaging for users with a different feature, create relevant recommendations for users’ playlists, and provide tools that allow users to control their screen time appropriately. These improvements are aimed at supporting user satisfaction, promoting personal growth, and balancing engagement and productivity.


Design Principle & Recommendation

Reward-Based Engagement

Introduce a reward system that incentivizes users to engage meaningfully with content by offering tangible benefits, such as ad-skipping options, in exchange for points earned through watching videos.
